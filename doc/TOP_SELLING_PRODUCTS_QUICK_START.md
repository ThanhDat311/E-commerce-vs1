### Filter by Date Range

**Option 2: Manual Entry**

### Filter by Category

**Steps**:

1. Click "Category" dropdown (first filter)
2. Select:
    - All Categories (default)
    - Electronics
    - Fashion
    - Home & Garden
    - Sports
3. Click "Apply" button
4. Table updates to show selected category only

---

### Change Sort Order

**Options**:

1. Click "Sort By" dropdown
2. Select:
    - **Units Sold** (default) - Most units first
    - **Revenue** - Highest revenue first
    - **Trend** - Fastest growing first
3. Click "Apply" button
4. Table re-ranks products

---

### Export Report

**Steps**:

1. Set filters as desired (date, category, sort)
2. Click blue "Export Report" button (top right)
3. CSV file downloads automatically
4. Filename: `top-products-YYYY-MM-DD-to-YYYY-MM-DD.csv`
5. Open in Excel or Google Sheets

**CSV Contains**:

- Product rank, name, category
- Units sold and revenue
- Average price per unit
- Trend percentage
- Share of total revenue

## üìà Understanding the Metrics

### Summary Cards

#### Card 1: Top Product Units

- **What It Shows**: How many units the #1 product sold
- **Why It Matters**: Volume indicates popularity
- **Example**: 1,245 units means best-seller had ~1,200 units
- **Trend**: 12% means 12% more than previous period

#### Card 2: Top Product Revenue

- **What It Shows**: Dollar amount from #1 product
- **Why It Matters**: Revenue shows financial impact
- **Example**: $45,230 = total dollars from top product
- **Trend**: 8% means 8% higher than previous period

#### Card 3: Products Tracked

- **What It Shows**: Total products in system
- **Why It Matters**: Context for how focused top 10 really are
- **Example**: 48 products total, showing top 10 = 21% of catalog
- **Note**: Top 10 drive most of the revenue

#### Card 4: Avg Product Revenue

- **What It Shows**: Average revenue per product
- **Why It Matters**: Shows how spread out revenue is
- **Example**: $3,245 average = moderate concentration
- **Trend**: -3% means average declining (concentration?)

## üìù Common Questions

### Q1: What time period should I analyze?

**A**: Start with:

- Last 7 days (trending)
- Last 30 days (patterns)
- Last 90 days (seasonal trends)
- Last year (comparisons)

### Q2: Why does the highest revenue product not have highest units?

**A**: Because price varies:

- Expensive items (headphones) = less units but more revenue
- Cheap items (screen protectors) = more units but less revenue

Example:

```
Premium Headphones: 1,245 units √ó $36 = $44,820
Screen Protectors: 2,345 units √ó $2 = $4,690
```

### Q3: How often should I check this?

**A**: Recommended cadence:

- Daily: Check for major changes or issues
- Weekly: Review trends and plan promotions
- Monthly: Analyze patterns and adjust strategy

### Q4: What do trends (‚Üë/‚Üì %) mean?

**A**: Comparing to identical previous period:

- ‚Üë 12% = 12% more than same period last time
- ‚Üì 8% = 8% less than same period last time
- Calculated automatically based on date range

### Q5: How do I use this for marketing?

**A**: Marketing actions by insight:

- **Top Performer**: Feature in ads
- **Growing**: Increase ad spend
- **Declining**: Run promotions
- **Category Leader**: Bundle with other products

### Q6: Can I compare two specific periods?

**A**: Yes:

1. Set date range for Period 1
2. Note the metrics
3. Set date range for Period 2
4. Compare visually
5. Or use export + spreadsheet for detailed comparison

### Q7: What if no data shows?

**A**: Checklist:

1. Verify date range has actual sales data
2. Try expanding to last 90 days
3. Check if you have order data in system
4. Try different category filter
5. Contact admin if still no data

## üÜò Troubleshooting

### Issue: No products showing

**Solutions**:

1. Verify date range includes data
2. Try wider date range (90 days)
3. Try "All Categories" filter
4. Check browser console for errors
5. Refresh page

### Issue: Trends show as 0%

**Cause**: No previous period data to compare
**Solution**: Use wider date range to ensure comparison period has data

### Issue: Percentages don't add to 100%

**Cause**: Showing only top 10, remaining products are excluded
**Solution**: That's normal - top 10 don't represent 100% of business

### Issue: Export file is empty

**Solutions**:

1. Verify filters show products in table
2. Try with "All Categories" filter
3. Refresh page and try again
4. Check if browser blocked download
